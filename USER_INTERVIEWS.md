# User Interview 1

## Person
A.K. — Solo SaaS Founder — Pre-revenue AI startup

## Direct Quotes
- “I honestly don’t know if my AI stack is reasonable or not.”
- “Every AI tool claims it saves money somehow.”
- “I pay for multiple tools because I’m afraid of losing productivity.”

## Most Surprising Insight
He cared less about absolute pricing and more about workflow disruption. Even small savings were ignored if switching tools felt risky.

## What It Changed
I added stronger reasoning explanations to recommendations instead of only showing savings numbers.

# User Interview 2

## Person
R.S. — Engineering Manager — 14-person startup

## Direct Quotes
- “We accidentally left unused AI seats active for months.”
- “Nobody owns AI tooling spend internally.”
- “Enterprise plans sound safer even when they’re unnecessary.”

## Most Surprising Insight
The company had no centralized visibility into AI subscriptions. Different teams purchased tools independently.

## What It Changed
I added team-size-aware downgrade logic and highlighted “enterprise overkill” recommendations more prominently.

# User Interview 3

## Person
M.P. — Freelance Developer + Agency Owner

## Direct Quotes
- “I compare tools based on vibe more than pricing.”
- “I’d definitely screenshot a report showing annual savings.”
- “Most AI pricing pages are confusing.”

## Most Surprising Insight
Visual presentation mattered more than expected. A clean, shareable report increased perceived trust dramatically.

## What It Changed
I redesigned the results page to emphasize:
- annual savings
- comparison clarity
- screenshot-friendly formatting
